Coffee

No need to stress or panic if your beloved coffee maker has broken down because with these four easy ways you can keep calm and brew on!

  • Brew up a Stovetop Cup
    1. Start with six ounces of cold water per serving. Then, pour water into a small saucepan over medium-high heat.
    2. Add 1 tablespoon of coffee per serving to the pan. If desired, add sugar now.
    3. Bring the coffee mixture to a boil.
    4. Remove from heat, let it sit for 4 minutes, then pour the coffee slowly and carefully from the pan so the grounds stay in the pan â€” and out of your cup(s).
  • Make Turkish Coffee
    1. Start with 6 ounces of cold water per serving. Then pour water into a small saucepan over low heat.
    2. Add 1 tablespoon of coffee per serving to the pan. If desired, add sugar now.
    3. When the water is just about to boil, remove it from the heat and skim off the foam. Place foam into your coffee cup(s).
    4. Return the pan to the heat and watch for it to get near a boil again, then remove from heat.
    5. Pour half of the coffee into your coffee cup(s). Return the pan to the heat once more. Boil for 15 additional seconds, then remove from heat.
  • Try a Faux French Press
    1. Boil water in a saucepan on the stove.
    2. In a deep bowl, add 1 tablespoon of coffee per serving.
    3. Pour a small amount of boiling water over the grounds to saturate them, and then add 6 ounces of water per serving.
    4. Use a spoon to press the coffee grounds to the bottom of the bowl.
    5. Pressing the spoon down on top of the grounds, pour the remaining liquid into your coffee cup
  • Create a Coffee Bag
    1. Lay one filter on the counter, and add 2 tablespoons of coffee.
    2. Bring the ends of the filter together and tie with twine.
    3. Saturate the bag of coffee in enough water just to soak the grounds, then let the grounds soak for 30 seconds.
    4. Pour 6 ounces of water into your coffee cup.
